About Lord Krishna

Lord Krishna is regarded as the eighth avatar of Lord Vishnu and one of the most worshipped deities in Hinduism. He was born in Mathura to Devaki and Vasudeva and was raised in Gokul by Yashoda and Nanda Maharaj.

From his childhood, Krishna displayed extraordinary divine powers. He protected people from evil forces, defeated powerful demons, and lifted the Govardhan Hill to save villagers from heavy rains. His playful childhood stories reflect joy, love, and divine charm.

As a great philosopher and spiritual guide, Krishna delivered the sacred Bhagavad Gita to Arjuna on the battlefield of Kurukshetra. His teachings emphasize Dharma (righteousness), Karma (duty), Bhakti (devotion), and selfless action.

Lord Krishna represents compassion, wisdom, courage, and leadership. His life teaches humanity to perform duties with dedication, remain calm during challenges, and always stand for truth and justice.
